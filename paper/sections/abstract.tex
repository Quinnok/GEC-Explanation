\begin{abstract}
Grammatical error correction (GEC) systems can generate natural-language explanations for their edits, but fluent explanations often copy the edited text, cite weak rules, omit contextual evidence, or rationalize invalid edits. We introduce RuleFaith-GEC, a verifier-guided pipeline for generating edit-level GEC explanations by decomposing quality into edit alignment, edit validity, rule correctness, evidence grounding, specificity, leakage, and selective abstention. The pipeline generates multiple teacher candidates, verifies each dimension, applies targeted repairs for failed dimensions, revalidates under target-masked controls, and selects or rejects the final explanation. Our human-adjudicated stress test over 160 edit-explanation items motivates this decomposition: reverse reconstruction obtains 0.789 binary faithfulness Macro-F1, but only 0.431 rule Macro-F1 and 0.426 evidence Macro-F1. In a Qwen3-8B local-teacher method pilot, RuleFaith gates reduce 160 candidates to 47 target-masked validated candidates, targeted repair recovers 16 additional rule/evidence candidates, and explicit Codex/AI pseudo-validation of the resulting 41-candidate package accepts 17, sends 13 for refinement, and rejects 11. These results support RuleFaith as a disciplined repair-and-selection framework, while showing that automatic gates alone are not sufficient for final human-grounded claims.
\end{abstract}
