\begin{abstract}
Grammatical error correction (GEC) systems increasingly provide natural-language explanations for their corrections, but a fluent explanation may fail to describe why a model produced a particular edit. This paper studies edit-level explanation faithfulness for model-produced GEC edits. We separate three questions that are often conflated: whether an explanation corresponds to the observed edit, whether it predicts the model's edit behavior under controlled input changes, and whether it states a valid grammar rule with appropriate evidence. We propose Counterfactual Edit Simulatability as the main behavioral test: construct GEC-specific counterfactual variants, rerun the original GEC model, and evaluate whether the explanation predicts whether the edit is preserved, changed, canceled, or retargeted.
