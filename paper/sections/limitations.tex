\section{Limitations}

Reverse Edit Reconstruction is a diagnostic for edit faithfulness, not a complete explanation-quality metric. It does not by itself establish grammatical validity, linguistic correctness, specificity, or human helpfulness. The approach depends on edit extraction quality, and ambiguous edit boundaries or multiple valid corrections can affect field-level scores. If explanations directly reveal the target edit, reconstruction may overestimate faithfulness by copying the answer. The method also depends on the capability and calibration of the reconstructor. Final experiments will require human evaluation to test whether reconstruction scores align with human judgments.
