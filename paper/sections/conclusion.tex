\section{Conclusion}

This paper reformulates edit-level GEC explanation work from a reconstruction-only diagnostic into RuleFaith-GEC, a verifier-guided repair and selection framework. Human-adjudicated stress-test labels show that reverse reconstruction is useful for edit alignment but insufficient for rule and evidence faithfulness. The Qwen3-8B method pilot shows how field-aware gating, target-masked validation, rule/evidence audit, and targeted repair can narrow a noisy teacher pool while exposing residual linguistic failures. The current evidence supports RuleFaith as a principled method direction and a reproducible pilot implementation. The next evidence gate is a blinded natural-explanation human evaluation followed by student distillation and selective abstention experiments; until then, Codex/AI pseudo-validation is treated only as internal triage, not as final human evidence.
